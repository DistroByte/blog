Home Assistant

Controlling the Apartment

You can control the lights and heating in the apartment with an app called Home Assistant.

Setup

  • Download the Home Assistant App from the Play Store (android) or the App Store (iOS).
  • Open the app, select "Enter Address Manually", and enter https://ha.dbyte.xyz into the prompt.
  • Log in with your credentials
    • Username: david, claire, maeve
    • Password hint: Camper 08
  • You only need to enable "notifications" for the app to work. All others can be set to "Don't allow".

Usage

You should see something like below when you get to the main screen. You can control individual lights, the boiler, and "scenes", which are pre-set light modes.

Tap on a switch to toggle that light or on a scene to enable that scene. The temperature sensors are just for viewing.

Notes

The boiler will turn off if it has been on for 3 hours and the living room temperature is above 22 degrees.

The boiler will send a notification to my phone when it has been on for 3 hours.

After 5 hours the boiler will turn off no matter what.

You can still manually control all of the items listed in the app:

  • The manual control panel on the wall acts as it always has. Boost for 1, 2 or 3 hours, or turn it off if it's currently on.
  • If the boiler is on (you can hear it), but the manual control panel on the wall says "Off", press the boost button once, wait 5 seconds, then press it again. You'll hear a click and the boiler will turn off.
